## Post Copy

Silicon Valley is very good at solving Silicon Valley problems.

AI-powered to-do lists. Chatbots for scheduling meetings. Another productivity tool for people who already have dozens.

We're interested in the other problems. The ones that affect billions but don't fit neatly into a pitch deck.

Here are 5 real problem areas AI4Change is collecting from communities worldwide:

1. CROP DISEASE PREDICTION
Farmers in sub-Saharan Africa lose up to 40% of crops to diseases detected too late. AI-powered image recognition on a smartphone camera could catch early signs — before visible symptoms appear to the human eye.

2. OFFLINE LITERACY TOOLS
A retired teacher in rural India wants to personalize reading lessons for 50 students with zero budget and no internet. Open-source language models running locally on cheap hardware could make adaptive learning accessible anywhere.

3. DISEASE OUTBREAK TRACKING
Community health workers still track outbreaks on paper in many regions. A simple mobile tool with AI-powered pattern detection could provide early warning signals that save weeks of response time.

4. AIR QUALITY MONITORING
A teenager in Sao Paulo wants to map air quality in her neighborhood. Low-cost IoT sensors paired with AI-driven analysis could turn a school project into public health infrastructure.

5. DISASTER RESPONSE COORDINATION
When floods hit, local responders know the terrain but lack real-time data. International teams have data but lack local knowledge. AI can bridge that gap — if someone builds the bridge.

These aren't hypothetical scenarios. They're real challenges from real communities.

At AI4Change, each problem that resonates becomes a candidate for our first "song" — a pilot project built by contributors from around the world, each using AI as their instrument.

Submit a problem your community faces: https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues/new

The community decides what gets built first. Not a board. Not a VC. Not an algorithm.
