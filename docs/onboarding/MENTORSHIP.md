# Mentorship Program

Pairing new contributors with experienced ones to make every journey from "interested" to "impactful" a guided one.

---

## Why Mentorship?

AI4Change is global, async, and open. That's powerful — but it can also be overwhelming for someone showing up for the first time. A mentor turns "where do I start?" into "let's start here."

The goal isn't to create dependency. It's to accelerate the path from newcomer to confident contributor.

---

## How It Works

### The Structure

| Element | Details |
|:--------|:--------|
| **Format** | 1-on-1 pairing — one mentor, one mentee |
| **Duration** | 4-week cycles, renewable by mutual agreement |
| **Communication** | Async-first (GitHub, email, chat). Sync calls optional. |
| **Commitment** | ~1-2 hours per week from each participant |
| **Matching** | Based on interests, skills, time zones, and goals |

### The Cycle

```
  Week 1: Get oriented — explore the codebase, docs, and community together
  Week 2: Find a contribution — identify a first issue or discussion to engage with
  Week 3: Build and submit — make the contribution with mentor support
  Week 4: Reflect and decide — retrospective, and decide whether to continue the pairing
```

---

## Mentor Role

### Who Can Be a Mentor?

Any active contributor who meets **all** of these:
- Has been active in AI4Change for at least **4 weeks**
- Has at least **3 merged PRs** or significant discussion participation
- Is willing to be patient, responsive, and supportive

### What Mentors Do

- **Respond to mentee questions within 48 hours** (async is fine)
- **Help find a first contribution** — point to `good first issue` labels, active discussions, or documentation needs
- **Share context** about AI4Change culture, workflow, and community norms
- **Review the mentee's first contribution** before submission (informal review, not gatekeeping)
- **Check in weekly** — a short async message is enough

### What Mentors Don't Do

- Pair-program for hours (unless you want to)
- Do the mentee's work for them
- Be available 24/7
- Have all the answers — "I don't know, let's find out" is a great response

### Tips for Effective Mentoring

1. **Ask before telling** — "What have you tried?" before "Here's the answer"
2. **Celebrate small wins** — a first PR, a first discussion comment, a first problem submission
3. **Be transparent about your own experience** — sharing your learning journey builds trust
4. **Point to the docs** — help the mentee learn to navigate, not just follow instructions
5. **Give honest feedback kindly** — "This is a good start, and here's how to make it stronger"

## Mentee Role

### Who Can Be a Mentee?

Anyone new to AI4Change. No experience requirements. If you're interested and willing to learn, you qualify.

### What Mentees Do

- **Come prepared** — read the [First Contribution guide](./FIRST-CONTRIBUTION.md) before your first check-in
- **Ask specific questions** — "How do I submit a PR to the problems repo?" beats "How do I contribute?"
- **Follow through** — make at least one contribution during the 4-week cycle
- **Respect your mentor's time** — they're volunteering; come organized to check-ins
- **Give feedback** — at the end of the cycle, share what worked and what didn't

### What Mentees Get

- A dedicated guide through the AI4Change ecosystem
- Faster path to meaningful contribution
- Connection to the broader community through your mentor's network
- Confidence that comes from supported first steps

## Matching Process

### How Matching Works

1. **Mentors register** by opening a Discussion with the `mentorship` tag, sharing:
   - Their areas of expertise (code, design, domain knowledge, community)
   - Time zone
   - Languages spoken
   - Availability (hours per week)

2. **Mentees register** the same way, sharing:
   - Their background and interests
   - What they hope to contribute
   - Time zone
   - Any preferences for a mentor's expertise

3. **Matching happens** based on:
   - **Skill alignment** — mentor expertise matches mentee interests
   - **Time zone overlap** — at least 3 hours of overlap for easier async
   - **Language** — shared language preferred, English as fallback
   - **Availability** — matching commitment levels

4. **Both parties confirm** before the pairing begins

### If the Match Isn't Working

No pairing is permanent. If either party feels the match isn't working:
- Talk to each other first — often a simple adjustment fixes things
- If that doesn't help, reach out to a Maintainer to request a re-match
- No hard feelings, no blame. Not every pairing clicks, and that's normal.

## Mentorship Tracks

Different mentees have different goals. Here are suggested tracks:

### Track 1: Problem Submitter

*For community members who want to contribute problems and context.*

| Week | Focus |
|:-----|:------|
| 1 | Understand the problem submission process and examples of great submissions |
| 2 | Draft a problem submission with mentor feedback |
| 3 | Submit and engage with community responses |
| 4 | Reflect; consider joining discussions on other submitted problems |

### Track 2: Solution Builder

*For developers and technical contributors.*

| Week | Focus |
|:-----|:------|
| 1 | Set up development environment; explore codebase and open issues |
| 2 | Pick a `good first issue` and start working on it |
| 3 | Submit PR; iterate on feedback |
| 4 | Reflect; identify next contribution area |

### Track 3: Documentation & Design

*For writers, editors, designers, and translators.*

| Week | Focus |
|:-----|:------|
| 1 | Review existing docs; identify gaps or improvements |
| 2 | Draft improvements or translations |
| 3 | Submit PR; iterate on feedback |
| 4 | Reflect; consider ongoing documentation ownership |

### Track 4: Community Ambassador

*For people who want to connect AI4Change with their communities.*

| Week | Focus |
|:-----|:------|
| 1 | Deep dive into AI4Change mission, manifesto, and talking points |
| 2 | Identify communities to connect with; draft outreach approach |
| 3 | Make first connection — share AI4Change, gather problems |
| 4 | Reflect; report back on community response and feedback |

How we know the program is working:

| Metric | Target |
|:-------|:-------|

| Mentees who remain active after their cycle | 60%+ |
