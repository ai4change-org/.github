### Tweet 2/10

A man named Roger Ridley stood on a street corner singing "Stand By Me." They recorded a guitarist in New Orleans, a drummer in Mumbai, a choir in Soweto.

None of them had met. But together, they made something no studio could produce alone.

---

### Tweet 3/10

That night, watching the video, one thought wouldn't let go:

"What if a farmer in Kenya, a data scientist in Berlin, and a designer in Lagos could build a solution the same way?"

Each using AI as their instrument. Together, creating something extraordinary.

---

### Tweet 4/10

AI is the great equalizer — if we let it be.

A teenager with a laptop can now do analysis that used to require a PhD. A farmer with a smartphone can access intelligence that Fortune 500 companies paid millions for.

The gap between expert and beginner has never been smaller.

---

### Tweet 5/10

But here's the problem: the people who need AI the most are the least likely to shape how it's used.

Every day we wait, someone builds another AI-powered to-do list while communities that could benefit most are left out.

---

### Tweet 6/10

AI4Change exists to close that gap.

We connect real people with real problems to global contributors who use AI to solve them. Open source. Community-driven. Free forever.

Every project is a "song." Every contributor is a musician.

#AI4Change

---

### Tweet 7/10

We're not a hackathon that builds for 48 hours and forgets.

We're not a nonprofit drowning in bureaucracy.

We're not an AI ethics org that writes papers.

We build the things AI should be used for. Starting with the problem, not the technology.

### Tweet 8/10

That's our first "song." The community decides what it will be.
