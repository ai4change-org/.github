## Post 1: The Founder's Story

**Title**: *Why I started AI4Change — and what Playing for Change taught me about building solutions*

---

It was late. The kind of late where the code has stopped making sense and you're three rabbit holes deep in YouTube.

Then I clicked on a video: "Stand By Me | Playing for Change | Song Around the World."

A man named Roger Ridley stood on a street corner in Santa Monica, singing with the kind of voice that makes you stop scrolling. But then the camera moved — to a guitarist in New Orleans, a drummer in Mumbai, a choir in Soweto. Each musician recorded independently, in their own city, on their own street.

None of them had ever met.

But layered together, the result was something impossible — a song no single studio, no single country, no single culture could have produced alone.

And I thought: **What if we could do this with solutions?**

Not music. Solutions. Real ones. For real problems.

A farmer in Kenya contributing local knowledge. A data scientist in Berlin building the model. A designer in Lagos making it usable. A student in Bogota testing it in her community.

Each working independently. Each using AI as their instrument. And together, creating something none of them could have built alone.

That thought became a note. Then a sketch. Then a GitHub organization.

**AI4Change — AI as the Great Equalizer.**

We're now collecting real-world problems from communities worldwide. Agriculture, education, health, environment, disaster response. Problems submitted by the people who live them — not sourced from think tanks.

The first "song" hasn't been written yet. That's the point. The community decides.

If you've ever thought "there has to be a better way" — this is the place where that feeling becomes a solution.

## Post 2: The First 5 Problems

**Title**: *These are the kinds of problems AI4Change exists to solve*

---

Silicon Valley is very good at solving Silicon Valley problems.

AI-powered to-do lists. Chatbots for scheduling meetings. Another productivity tool for people who already have dozens.

We're interested in the other problems. The ones that affect billions but don't fit neatly into a pitch deck.

Here are 5 real problem areas AI4Change is collecting from communities worldwide:

**1. Crop Disease Prediction**
Farmers in sub-Saharan Africa lose up to 40% of crops to diseases detected too late. AI-powered image recognition running on a smartphone camera could catch early signs — before visible symptoms appear to the human eye.

**2. Offline Literacy Tools**
A retired teacher in rural India wants to personalize reading lessons for 50 students with zero budget and no internet. Open-source language models running locally on cheap hardware could make adaptive learning accessible anywhere.

**3. Disease Outbreak Tracking**
Community health workers still track outbreaks on paper in many regions. A simple mobile tool with AI-powered pattern detection could provide early warning signals that save weeks of response time.

**4. Air Quality Monitoring**
A teenager in Sao Paulo wants to map air quality in her neighborhood. Low-cost IoT sensors + AI-driven analysis could turn her school project into public health infrastructure.

**5. Disaster Response Coordination**
When floods hit, local responders know the terrain but lack real-time data. When aid arrives, international teams have data but lack local knowledge. AI can bridge that gap — if someone builds the bridge.

These aren't hypothetical. They're real challenges from real communities.

At AI4Change, we don't start with a solution looking for a problem. We start with the problem and let the community shape the solution.

Each problem that resonates becomes a candidate for our first "song" — a pilot project built by contributors from around the world, each using AI as their instrument.

**Submit a problem your community faces**: github.com/ai4change-org/problems

## Post 3: Call for Contributors

**Title**: *We're looking for people who build things that matter*

---

AI4Change is open source and community-driven. We need people — and you don't need to be a developer.

Here's who we're looking for:

**Problem Submitters**
You live in a community facing a real challenge. Maybe it's agriculture, health, education, or the environment. You don't need a solution — you need to describe the problem. Your lived experience is the most valuable contribution.

**Solution Builders**
Developers, data scientists, ML engineers. You build things. You want those things to matter. You'll use AI as your multiplier — writing code, training models, building tools — alongside contributors from around the world.

**Designers**
UX/UI designers who care about accessibility and real-world usability. The solutions we build need to work for a farmer on a low-cost phone, not just a developer on a MacBook.

**Storytellers**
Writers, filmmakers, documentarians. Every AI4Change project is a "Song Around the World" — and someone needs to tell the story of how strangers from different continents built something together.

**Domain Experts**
Agronomists, public health professionals, educators, disaster response specialists. Your expertise shapes how AI is applied — you keep solutions grounded in reality.

**Translators**
Our community spans the globe. Translating documentation, problems, and solutions into your local language removes barriers for the next contributor.

**How to get started:**

1. Visit our GitHub: github.com/ai4change-org
2. Read the manifesto to understand the why
3. Submit a problem or join a discussion
4. Star and share — help us reach the people who need to see this

We're in our founding phase. The first "song" — our first pilot project — will be chosen by the community. Every early contributor shapes what this becomes.

You don't need permission. You need a problem, a skill, or the desire to build something that matters.
