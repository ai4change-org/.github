# Community Guidelines

How we work together at AI4Change.

---

## How to Participate

### GitHub Discussions

[GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/orgs/ai4change-org/discussions) is our primary community space. Use it for:

- **Ideas** — propose new approaches, tools, or initiatives
- **Questions** — ask about AI4Change, how to contribute, or technical topics
- **Show & Tell** — share what you're building, learning, or experimenting with
- **Problem Discussion** — dive deeper into submitted problems before they become projects

### Discussion Norms

- **Lead with curiosity, not judgment** — "Tell me more about that" beats "That won't work"
- **Be specific** — "This could help farmers in arid regions because..." beats "Great idea!"
- **Credit sources** — link to research, data, or prior art when referencing it
- **Stay on topic** — keep threads focused; open a new discussion for tangential ideas
- **Respect time zones** — our community spans the globe; async-first is the default

## Submitting Problems

The heart of AI4Change is the [Problems repository](https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues). Anyone, anywhere can submit a real-world challenge their community faces.

### What Makes a Good Problem Submission?

- **Specific** — "Farmers in my district lose 30% of crops to late blight detection" not "Agriculture needs AI"
- **Grounded in experience** — you've seen it, lived it, or talked to people who have
- **Scoped** — solvable or improvable, not "fix global poverty"
- **Context-rich** — location, who's affected, what's been tried, what tools are available

### Problem Lifecycle

```
  Submit ──> Community Discussion ──> Signal (upvotes, comments)
                                            │
                                            ▼
                                     Trending Board
                                            │
                                            ▼
                                   Candidate for "Song"
                                            │
                                            ▼
                                   Community Vote ──> Pilot Project
```

## Proposing a New Project ("Song")

When a problem has gathered enough community signal (discussion, upvotes, diverse interest), it can be proposed as a **"song"** — an AI4Change pilot project.

### Proposal Requirements

1. **Problem reference** — link to the original problem issue
2. **Why now** — what makes this solvable today with AI
3. **Who benefits** — specific communities, with rough scale of impact
4. **Initial approach** — not a full architecture, just a credible starting direction
5. **Skill needs** — what kinds of contributors would be needed
6. **AI angle** — how AI serves as the multiplier, not just the buzzword

### Selection Process

1. Proposal posted as a Discussion with the `song-proposal` tag
2. 14-day community comment and refinement period
3. Community vote (all contributors eligible)
4. Steward ratification (see [Governance](../GOVERNANCE.md))
5. Project kickoff with a public project charter

## Regional Hubs

AI4Change is global by design. As the community grows, we'll support **regional hubs** — self-organized groups of contributors who share a geographic context.

### What Is a Regional Hub?

A regional hub is a group of AI4Change contributors in the same country or region who:

- Meet (virtually or in person) to discuss local problems
- Translate and localize AI4Change resources for their context
- Advocate for problems specific to their region
- Mentor new contributors in their area

### Starting a Hub

1. **Find 3+ people** in your region interested in AI4Change
2. **Open a Discussion** with the `regional-hub` tag describing your region and goals
3. **Get recognized** — once active, your hub gets listed on the website and a dedicated Discussion category
4. **Stay connected** — hubs share updates monthly in a cross-hub sync thread

### Planned Hub Support (Phase 4+)

- Dedicated channels per hub
- Regional problem aggregation
- Localized content and documentation
- Hub representation on the Community Council

## Translation & Localization

We want AI4Change to be accessible regardless of language.

### Principles

- **English is the common language** for code, core docs, and cross-hub communication
- **Translations are welcomed** for all community-facing content (manifesto, website, guides)
- **Local context matters** — translations should adapt examples and references, not just words
- **Machine translation is a starting point** — AI tools can draft, but human reviewers ensure quality and cultural fit

### How to Contribute Translations

1. Check if a translation exists for your language (look for `lang/<code>/` directories)
2. If not, open a Discussion with the `translation` tag proposing your language
3. Fork the relevant repo, create translations in the appropriate directory
4. Submit a PR — tag it with `translation` and your language code
5. Another speaker of that language reviews before merge

### Priority Languages (Community-Driven)

We'll prioritize translations based on where our community and problems are. No languages are excluded — all are welcome.

## Mentorship Program

### Vision

Every new contributor should have a path from "interested" to "impactful." The mentorship program pairs experienced contributors with newcomers.

### How It Will Work (Launching Phase 2)

| Role | Description |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **Mentor** | An active contributor with 3+ merged PRs or ongoing discussion participation |
| **Mentee** | Anyone new to AI4Change who wants guidance |
| **Match** | Based on interests, skills, and time zones |
| **Duration** | 4-week cycles, renewable |

### Mentor Responsibilities

- Respond to mentee questions within 48 hours (async is fine)
- Help mentee find a first contribution (problem submission, discussion, code)
- Share context about AI4Change culture and workflow
- No obligation to pair-program or commit specific hours — just be available

### Mentee Expectations

- Come with curiosity and a willingness to learn
- Respect your mentor's time — come prepared with specific questions
- Make at least one contribution during the mentorship cycle
- Share feedback at the end to help improve the program

### Signing Up

The mentorship program will launch during Phase 2. To express interest:
- Open a Discussion with the `mentorship` tag
- Indicate whether you'd like to be a mentor, mentee, or both
- Share your interests and timezone

## Community Health

### What We Value

- **Inclusion** — everyone's perspective has value, especially those closest to the problems
- **Patience** — people contribute across time zones, languages, and skill levels
- **Substance over volume** — one thoughtful contribution beats ten drive-by comments
- **Kindness** — we disagree on approaches, never on each other's worth

### What We Don't Tolerate

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Gatekeeping ("you're not technical enough to contribute")
- Dismissing problems because they don't affect you personally
- Spamming, self-promotion, or off-topic content

### Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness behavior that violates these guidelines:
1. Report it via a private message to a maintainer
2. Or email conduct@ai4change.org (monitored by the steward)
3. All reports are handled confidentially
