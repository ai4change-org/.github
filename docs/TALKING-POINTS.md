# AI4Change Talking Points

Key messages, elevator pitches, FAQ, and comparison framework for interviews, podcasts, and press.

---

## Elevator Pitches

### 30 Seconds

AI4Change is an open-source movement inspired by Playing for Change — the project that connected street musicians across continents into songs no single studio could produce. We do the same thing, but with AI and solutions. Real communities submit real problems, global contributors use AI as their instrument to solve them, and everything ships free and open source. Every project is a "song." Every contributor is a musician.

### 60 Seconds

You know Playing for Change? They recorded musicians independently in cities across the world — a guitarist in New Orleans, a drummer in Mumbai, a choir in Soweto — and mixed them into one song. None of them had met. The result was extraordinary.

AI4Change applies that exact model to solving real-world problems with AI. A farmer in Kenya contributes local knowledge. A data scientist in Berlin builds the model. A designer in Lagos makes it usable. Each uses AI as their instrument. Together, they create open-source solutions no single person or institution could build alone.

We're not a hackathon, not a nonprofit, not an AI ethics paper. We build the things AI should be used for — starting with the problem, not the technology. Everything is open, free, and community-driven.

### 2 Minutes

Late one night, I was watching Playing for Change's "Stand By Me" on YouTube. A man named Roger Ridley stood on a street corner in Santa Monica, singing with a voice that stops you cold. Then the camera moved — to a guitarist in New Orleans, a drummer in Mumbai, a choir in Soweto. Musicians who had never met, each recorded independently, blended into a song no single studio could produce.

And I thought: what if we could do this with solutions?

AI is the most powerful tool humanity has ever created. A teenager with a laptop can now do analysis that used to require a PhD and a six-figure budget. But the people who need AI the most — a farmer watching crops fail, a health worker tracking outbreaks on paper, a teacher trying to personalize education with zero budget — they're not at the table. They're not in the boardrooms or the research labs or the Y Combinator demo days.

AI4Change exists to close that gap. We're an open-source movement where real communities submit real problems, and global contributors use AI as their instrument to solve them together. We call each project a "song." The code is the melody, the data is the rhythm, the documentation is the harmony, and the contributors are the musicians.

We're different from hackathons because we build to last — continuous, open, evolving. Different from nonprofits because you need a GitHub account and willingness, not a grant cycle. Different from AI ethics organizations because we don't write papers about what AI should do — we build the things.

Right now we're in Phase 1: collecting seed problems from communities worldwide. The community will vote on the first "song" — the first pilot project. Our goal is to prove that strangers from different continents, each using AI as their multiplier, can build something together that none of them could build alone. Just like Playing for Change proved it with music.

## Key Messages

Use these as anchor points in any conversation. Return to them when the discussion drifts.

### The Core Idea

> "Playing for Change connected musicians across continents to create songs no studio could. AI4Change connects problem-solvers across continents to create solutions no institution could."

> "AI is the great equalizer — if we let it be. The gap between expert and beginner has never been smaller. But the people who need AI the most are the least likely to shape how it's used. We're closing that gap while it's still possible."

### The Model

> "We don't start with technology. We start with the problem. Real communities submit real challenges. Global contributors use AI as their instrument to build solutions. Everything ships open source, documented, and free."

### The Differentiation

> "We're not a hackathon that builds for 48 hours and forgets. We're not a nonprofit drowning in bureaucracy. We're not an AI ethics org that writes papers. We build the things AI should be used for."

### The Ask

> "You don't need to be an AI expert. You don't need to be a developer. You need a problem. Submit one. Or use your skills to help solve one. Every solution starts with a problem."

## FAQ for Press

### What is AI4Change?

An open-source movement that connects real-world problems — submitted by communities — with global contributors who use AI as their instrument to build solutions together. Inspired by Playing for Change.

### Who founded it and why?

It started with a developer watching Playing for Change's "Stand By Me" and realizing the same model — ordinary people across continents, each contributing their part, creating something extraordinary together — could work for AI-powered solutions.

### How does it work?

1. Anyone submits a problem their community faces (via GitHub Issues)
2. The community discusses and signals which problems resonate most
3. The most compelling problems become candidates for "songs" (pilot projects)
4. Global contributors join — developers, designers, domain experts, storytellers
5. Each uses AI as their multiplier
6. The solution ships open source, documented, and free
7. The journey is documented as a "Song Around the World"

### What's the business model?

There isn't one in the traditional sense. AI4Change is open source and community-driven. Long-term sustainability will come from grants, sponsorships, and donations — not from monetizing the solutions, which are and will remain free.

### What stage are you at?

Phase 1: Foundation. The organization, documentation, website, and problem submission pipeline are live. We're collecting the first wave of seed problems from communities worldwide. Phase 2 (Community) begins in April 2026.

### What kinds of problems are you looking for?

Real, specific, grounded problems from people who live them. Agriculture, education, health, environment, disaster response — any area where AI can be a multiplier. "Farmers in my district lose 30% of crops to late blight" is a great problem. "Fix agriculture" is not.

### How is this different from [X]?

See comparison table below.

### Can companies get involved?

Yes — as contributors, sponsors, or partners. But AI4Change is community-governed, not corporate-steered. Company involvement is welcome when it serves the mission, not the brand.

### What technology stack do you use?

GitHub for everything — Issues for problems, Discussions for community, Pages for the website. Project-specific stacks will be chosen by each project team. The emphasis is on accessibility (works offline, works on low-end devices, open source).

### How do you prevent "AI solutionism" — throwing AI at problems that don't need it?

Every project starts with the problem, not the technology. The community vets whether AI is genuinely the right multiplier. If the best solution doesn't need AI, that's fine too. The "AI" in AI4Change is about using the most powerful tool available, not about forcing it where it doesn't belong.

### What's the Playing for Change connection? Are you affiliated?

AI4Change is inspired by Playing for Change's model — not affiliated with them. They proved that ordinary people, connected by a shared purpose, can produce extraordinary things. We're applying that proof to AI-powered problem-solving.

## Interview Tips

### Opening Hooks

- "Have you seen Playing for Change's 'Stand By Me'? If not, watch it tonight. It'll change how you think about collaboration."
- "What if the most impactful AI product of the next decade isn't built by a company — but by a farmer and a data scientist who've never met?"
- "AI is the great equalizer. But only if we let it be."

### Stories to Tell

1. **The origin story**: Watching Playing for Change late at night, the tears, the thought that wouldn't let go
2. **The farmer analogy**: A farmer in Kenya with a smartphone can now access intelligence that Fortune 500 companies paid millions to build
3. **The gap**: Every day someone builds another AI-powered to-do list while communities that could benefit most are left out
4. **The vision**: A crop disease predictor built by a Kenyan farmer, a Brazilian data scientist, and a French agronomist — all open source

### Questions to Redirect

| If they ask... | Redirect to... |
|:---------------|:---------------|
| "Isn't this just another hackathon?" | "Hackathons build for 48 hours and forget. We build to last." |
| "How do you make money?" | "We don't. The solutions are free. The sustainability comes from grants and community." |
| "Isn't AI overhyped?" | "AI hype is about chatbots and image generators. AI impact is about a farmer predicting crop disease." |
| "Can this really scale?" | "Playing for Change scaled to 60+ countries with musicians. We're applying the same model." |
| "What if the problems are too hard?" | "We don't pretend we'll solve everything. We start with one problem, one 'song,' and prove the model works." |
