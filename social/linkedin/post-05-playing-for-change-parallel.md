## Post Copy

In 2005, a filmmaker named Mark Johnson walked through Santa Monica and heard Roger Ridley singing "Stand By Me" on a street corner. He had an audacious idea: record musicians around the world, each playing their part of the same song, and blend them into one recording.

He didn't build a record label. He didn't hire session musicians. He went to street corners, villages, and market squares. He found people who played because they had to — because music was as necessary as breathing.

The result was Playing for Change — a movement that has connected thousands of musicians across 60+ countries and built music schools worldwide.

They proved something radical: when you remove the barriers between people, they create things that transcend what any individual or institution could produce.

AI4Change is built on that exact blueprint. Here's how the parallel works:

Playing for Change starts with the music, not the business model.
AI4Change starts with the problem, not the technology.

PFC says the musician on the street matters as much as the one in the studio.
We say the farmer in the field matters as much as the developer at the keyboard.

PFC has each person play their instrument; the song needs all of them.
We have each person use AI their way; the solution needs all of them.

PFC records the faces, the streets, the stories — not just the audio.
We document the communities, the contexts, the journeys — not just the code.

Playing for Change didn't just create beautiful music. They created proof that ordinary people, connected by a shared purpose, can produce extraordinary things.

We're creating the same proof. With solutions instead of songs. With AI instead of instruments. With GitHub instead of recording equipment.

The first "song" hasn't been chosen yet. The community decides what gets built. And we document every step — because the story of how strangers from different continents build something together using AI is as powerful as the solution itself.

Watch the original video that started it all. Then imagine what we could build together.
