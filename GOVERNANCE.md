# Governance

How decisions are made at AI4Change.

---

## Current Model: Benevolent Steward

AI4Change is in its founding phase. During this period, the project operates under a **Benevolent Steward** model — a single maintainer makes final decisions, with strong community input through GitHub Discussions.

### Who Is the Steward?

The founding maintainer of AI4Change. All significant decisions are made transparently in public GitHub Discussions, and the steward commits to:

- **Explaining every significant decision** publicly, with reasoning
- **Seeking community input** before making decisions that affect contributors
- **Never overriding clear community consensus** without compelling reason and public explanation
- **Working toward distributing governance** as the community grows

### How Decisions Are Made Today

| Decision Type | Process |
|:-------------|:--------|
| **Day-to-day** (issue triage, PR reviews, docs) | Steward or any active maintainer |
| **Project direction** (which problem to tackle, phase transitions) | Community Discussion + steward decision |
| **New "song" (pilot project) selection** | Community vote with steward ratification |
| **Policy changes** (governance, code of conduct, brand) | RFC in Discussions, 7-day comment period, steward decision |
| **New maintainers** | Steward nomination after consistent contribution |

### Decision Principles

1. **Default to open** — discussions, decisions, and reasoning happen in public
2. **Community-first** — the people closest to the problems have the most say
3. **Rough consensus** — we seek broad agreement, not unanimous votes
4. **Working code wins** — a running prototype speaks louder than a proposal
5. **Reversible decisions move fast** — only irreversible decisions need deep deliberation

## Planned: Community Council

As AI4Change grows beyond its founding phase, governance will evolve:

### Phase 2: Advisory Circle (Target: Q3 2026)

- **3-5 active contributors** invited to form an Advisory Circle
- The Circle provides input on project selection, priorities, and community norms
- Steward retains final decision authority but commits to following Circle consensus in most cases
- Members serve 6-month renewable terms

### Phase 3: Community Council (Target: Q1 2027)

- **Elected council** of 5-7 members representing different regions and skill sets
- Council makes decisions by majority vote on:
  - Project selection and priorities
  - Policy changes
  - New maintainer approvals
  - Budget allocation (if applicable)
- Steward transitions to a council seat with no special veto power
- Elections held every 6 months, open to all contributors with 3+ merged PRs or 3+ accepted problem submissions

### Guiding Principle

> Governance should be as open as the code. The goal is not to accumulate authority — it's to distribute it as quickly as the community can sustain it.

## Contributing to Governance

Want to propose a change to how AI4Change is governed?

1. Open a Discussion with the `governance` tag
2. Describe the change and why it matters
3. Allow 7 days for community comment
4. The steward (or future council) will make a decision and post the rationale

## Code of Conduct

All participants in AI4Change spaces are expected to be respectful, constructive, and inclusive. A full Code of Conduct will be adopted during Phase 2 — for now, the standard is simple:

**Be kind. Be helpful. Center the communities we're here to serve.**
