## Post Copy

Here's a question that keeps me up at night:

Why does Silicon Valley keep building AI-powered to-do lists while a farmer in sub-Saharan Africa loses 40% of her crops to diseases she could detect with a smartphone camera?

The answer isn't technology. The technology exists. The answer is access.

AI4Change is an open-source movement designed to close that gap. Here's how it works:

1. Real people submit real problems their communities face — via GitHub Issues
2. The global community discusses, refines, and signals which problems matter most
3. The most compelling problems become pilot projects — we call them "songs"
4. Contributors from around the world join: developers, designers, domain experts, storytellers
5. Each uses AI as their multiplier
6. The solution ships — open source, documented, free forever

Think of it as Playing for Change meets open-source development. PFC recorded street musicians across 60+ countries and blended them into songs no single studio could produce. We're doing the same thing — with solutions instead of songs, AI instead of instruments.

The code is the melody. The data is the rhythm. The documentation is the harmony. The contributors are the musicians. And the audience is every community that needs a solution but was never invited to the table.

We don't start with technology looking for a problem. We start with the problem and let the community shape the solution.

Our first "song" hasn't been chosen yet. That's the point — the community decides.
