## 1. LinkedIn Founding Story Post

> **Copy the text below and post on LinkedIn.**

---

Late one night, three rabbit holes deep in YouTube, I clicked on Playing for Change's "Stand By Me."

A man named Roger Ridley stood on a street corner in Santa Monica, singing with the kind of voice that makes you stop scrolling. Then the camera moved — to a guitarist in New Orleans. A drummer in Mumbai. A choir in Soweto. Musicians who had never met, each recorded independently, blended into a song no single studio could produce.

I sat there, headphones on, tears forming, and thought: "What if we could do this with solutions?"

Not music. Solutions. Real ones. A farmer in Kenya contributing local knowledge. A data scientist in Berlin building the model. A designer in Lagos making it usable. A student in Bogota testing it in her community. Each using AI as their instrument — together creating something none of them could build alone.

That thought became AI4Change.

AI is the most powerful tool humanity has ever created. A teenager with a laptop can now do analysis that used to require a PhD and a six-figure budget. But the people who need AI the most are the least likely to shape how it's used.

We're changing that. AI4Change is an open-source movement connecting real people with real problems to global contributors who use AI as their instrument. Every project is a "song." Every contributor is a musician. Every solution is open, free, and built by the world.

We're collecting our first problems now — from farmers, teachers, health workers, and community leaders worldwide.

Have a problem your community faces? Submit it: https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues/new

Want to watch this grow? Star us on GitHub: https://github.com/ai4change-org

Every solution starts with a problem. What's yours?

## 4. Hacker News Post

### Title

Show HN: AI4Change — Playing for Change meets open-source AI for real-world problems

### Comment Body

> **Post the title as a link to `https://github.com/ai4change-org`, then post this as the first comment.**

---

Hey HN,

I was watching Playing for Change's "Stand By Me" late one night — the video where they record musicians independently across the world and mix them into one song. A guitarist in New Orleans, a drummer in Mumbai, a choir in Soweto. None of them had met, but the result was something no single studio could produce.

I thought: what if we could do this with solutions?

A farmer in Kenya contributing local knowledge. A data scientist in Berlin building the model. A designer in Lagos making it usable. Each using AI as their instrument — together creating something none of them could build alone.

That's AI4Change.

**What it is:** An open-source movement that connects real-world problems (submitted by communities) with global contributors who use AI to solve them. We call each project a "song."

**How it works:**
1. Anyone submits a problem (via GitHub Issues)
2. Community discusses, signals what matters most
3. Trending problems become candidates for "songs" (pilot projects)
4. Contributors from around the world join — developers, designers, domain experts, storytellers
5. Each uses AI as their multiplier
6. Solution ships open source, documented, free

**Why now:** A teenager with a laptop and an LLM can build tools that rival enterprise software. The gap between expert and beginner has never been smaller. But the people who need AI the most — farmers, teachers, health workers — aren't at the table. We want to change that before the window closes.

**What makes it different from hackathons:** We build to last. Continuous, evolving, community-driven. Not 48-hour throwaway projects.

**What makes it different from nonprofits:** You need a GitHub account and willingness, not a grant cycle and a board.

**What makes it different from AI ethics orgs:** We build the things. Not papers about what should be built.

**Current status:** Phase 1 (Foundation). We're collecting seed problems from communities worldwide. The org, docs, website, and problem submission pipeline are live. Looking for our first 10 problems from 5+ countries.

**Stack:** GitHub for everything — Issues for problems, Discussions for community, GitHub Pages for the website. Intentionally low-ceremony.

Would love feedback on the model, the approach, and whether this resonates. And if you have a problem your community faces — submit it. That's the most important contribution right now.

GitHub: https://github.com/ai4change-org
Submit a problem: https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues/new
Manifesto: https://github.com/ai4change-org/.github/blob/main/docs/MANIFESTO.md
