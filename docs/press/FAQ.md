# Frequently Asked Questions

## About AI4Change

### What is AI4Change?

AI4Change is an open-source initiative that connects communities facing real-world problems with global contributors who build AI-powered solutions — together, in the open, free forever. Inspired by [Playing for Change](https://playingforchange.com), we call each project a "song" and every contributor a "musician."

### Is AI4Change a nonprofit?

Not currently. AI4Change is a community-driven open-source initiative operating on GitHub. We don't have a formal legal entity yet. As the initiative grows, we're exploring the right structure — whether that's a nonprofit, a fiscal sponsorship arrangement, or a foundation. The commitment that won't change: **all solutions are open source and free forever.**

### Who is behind AI4Change?

AI4Change was started by a developer who was inspired by Playing for Change's model of global collaboration. It is maintained by a founding steward with the explicit goal of distributing governance to the community as it grows. See our [Governance document](https://github.com/ai4change-org/.github/blob/main/GOVERNANCE.md) for details.

### Is AI4Change affiliated with Playing for Change?

No. AI4Change is an independent initiative inspired by Playing for Change's model and philosophy. We deeply respect their work and credit them as our inspiration. We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Playing for Change or the Playing for Change Foundation.

## Funding & Sustainability

### How is AI4Change funded?

Currently, AI4Change operates at zero cost beyond volunteer time. All infrastructure is free (GitHub, GitHub Pages, GitHub Actions). As the initiative grows, we plan to explore:

- **GitHub Sponsors** — individual and corporate sponsorships
- **Grants** — from foundations aligned with open source, AI for good, or social impact
- **Donations** — from individuals who believe in the mission

We will never charge for solutions, gate contributions behind paywalls, or accept funding that compromises our open-source commitment.

### Where does the money go?

Currently, there are no expenses. Future funding would support:

- Infrastructure costs (compute for AI model training, hosting beyond GitHub Pages)
- Community programs (mentorship, regional hub support, translation efforts)
- Storytelling and documentation (filming "Song Around the World" videos)
- Event participation and community building

Financial decisions will be made transparently and reported publicly.

### Can I sponsor or donate?

Yes. We accept sponsorships through [GitHub Sponsors](https://github.com/sponsors/ai4change-org) and other platforms listed in our [FUNDING.yml](https://github.com/ai4change-org/.github/blob/main/FUNDING.yml). All contributions go directly to supporting the initiative.

## Contributing

### Do I need to be a developer to contribute?

Absolutely not. AI4Change needs:

| Role | What You Do |
|:-----|:-----------|
| **Problem Submitters** | Share real challenges your community faces |
| **Solution Builders** | Developers, data scientists, ML engineers who write code |
| **Designers** | UX/UI designers focused on accessibility and real-world usability |
| **Domain Experts** | Agronomists, health workers, educators who ground solutions in reality |
| **Storytellers** | Writers and filmmakers who document the journey |
| **Translators** | People who make content accessible in their language |
| **Ambassadors** | People who spread the word and connect communities |

The most important contribution is a problem. Your lived experience is more valuable than any line of code.

### How do I get started?

1. **Visit GitHub**: [github.com/ai4change-org](https://github.com/ai4change-org)
2. **Read the manifesto**: Understand the "why" behind the "what"
3. **Pick your path**:
   - **Submit a problem**: [github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues/new](https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues/new)
   - **Join the discussion**: [github.com/orgs/ai4change-org/discussions](https://github.com/orgs/ai4change-org/discussions)
   - **Star and share**: Help us reach more people

### Do I need a GitHub account?

Yes, for now. GitHub is our primary platform for collaboration. Creating an account is free and takes 2 minutes. In the future, we may add additional channels for problem submission (email, mobile forms) to lower the barrier further.

### What programming languages/frameworks do you use?

There's no single tech stack. Each pilot project ("song") will choose its own technology based on the problem requirements. Factors that matter most:

- **Accessibility**: Can it run on low-cost devices?
- **Offline capability**: Does it work without reliable internet?
- **Maintainability**: Can the community sustain it long-term?
- **AI integration**: What models and tools best serve this specific problem?

### Is there a Code of Conduct?

Yes. We follow the [Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct](https://github.com/ai4change-org/.github/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). The short version: be kind, be helpful, center the communities we're here to serve.

## Projects & Problems

### What kinds of problems can I submit?

Real challenges that your community faces and that AI could help address. Good submissions are:

- **Specific** — "Farmers in my district lose 30% of crops to late blight" not "Agriculture needs AI"
- **Grounded** — based on lived experience, not theoretical
- **Scoped** — improvable, not "fix global poverty"
- **Context-rich** — location, who's affected, what's been tried

### How are problems selected to become projects?

1. Problems are submitted via GitHub Issues
2. The community discusses and refines them
3. Problems with strong signal (discussion, upvotes, diverse interest) become candidates
4. The community votes on which problem becomes the next "song"
5. The steward (or future council) ratifies the selection

### What is a "song"?

A "song" is our term for a pilot project — an AI-powered solution built collaboratively by contributors from around the world. The term comes from our Playing for Change inspiration: just as musicians from different continents each play their part of the same song, contributors from different backgrounds each build their part of the same solution.

### When will the first project launch?

The first "song" is planned for **Phase 3** of our roadmap (May-June 2026). The community will vote on which problem to tackle first during Phase 2 (April 2026). See the full [Roadmap](https://github.com/ai4change-org/.github/blob/main/docs/ROADMAP.md).

## Governance & Operations

### How are decisions made?

AI4Change currently operates under a **Benevolent Steward** model — a founding maintainer makes final decisions with strong community input through GitHub Discussions. The plan is to transition to an elected Community Council as the initiative grows. All significant decisions are made transparently in public.

See [Governance](https://github.com/ai4change-org/.github/blob/main/GOVERNANCE.md) for full details.

### Is AI4Change a global initiative?

Yes. AI4Change is designed to be global from day one. We prioritize:

- **Async-first communication** across time zones
- **Translation and localization** of community-facing content
- **Regional hubs** — self-organized groups of contributors sharing a geographic context
- **Inclusive design** — solutions that work on low-cost devices with limited connectivity

### How do you handle data privacy?

All AI4Change projects must follow responsible data practices:

- No personal data collection without explicit consent
- Problem submissions are public by default (submitters can request anonymity)
- Any datasets used in projects must be ethically sourced and documented
- We follow applicable data protection regulations (GDPR, etc.)
- See our [Security Policy](https://github.com/ai4change-org/.github/blob/main/SECURITY.md) for vulnerability reporting
