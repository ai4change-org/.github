# Community Roles

AI4Change is built by its community. Everyone starts as a contributor, and roles evolve naturally as you participate. There's no application process — roles are earned through consistent, quality contribution.

---

## Role Overview

```
  Newcomer ──> Problem Submitter ──> Solution Builder ──> Reviewer ──> Maintainer
                                                                          │
                                                                          ▼
                                                                      Ambassador
```

All roles are **additive** — becoming a Reviewer doesn't mean you stop submitting problems. And every role is equally important: a Problem Submitter who deeply understands their community is as valuable as a Maintainer who reviews code.

---

## Problem Submitter

> *The heart of AI4Change. Every solution starts here.*

### What You Do

- Submit real-world problems your community faces via [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/ai4change-org/problems/issues/new)
- Provide rich context: who's affected, what's been tried, what tools are available
- Engage in discussions about submitted problems
- Help validate proposed solutions against real-world constraints
- Connect AI4Change with the communities you serve

### How to Start

Submit your first problem. That's it. You're a Problem Submitter.

### Recognition

- Listed as problem originator on any project born from your submission
- Acknowledged in the "Song Around the World" story for your project
- Badge in GitHub Discussions (once badges are implemented)

### What Great Looks Like

A Problem Submitter who regularly contributes context-rich, specific problems from their community. Who stays engaged when those problems are discussed. Who bridges the gap between the people living the problem and the people building the solution.

## Solution Builder

> *The hands that build the songs.*

### What You Do

- Contribute code, data analysis, design, or documentation to active projects
- Use AI tools as your instrument — documenting how you use them to multiply your impact
- Collaborate with contributors across time zones and skill levels
- Follow project conventions and submit clean, reviewed Pull Requests
- Help with code reviews for other Solution Builders

### Prerequisites

- GitHub account
- At least one meaningful contribution (PR merged, design submitted, or significant documentation)
- Familiarity with the project's contribution guidelines

### How to Start

1. Find a `good first issue` in any [AI4Change repo](https://github.com/ai4change-org)
2. Comment to signal you're working on it
3. Submit a Pull Request
4. Once merged — you're a Solution Builder

### Recognition

- Listed as a contributor on the project
- Credited in release notes
- Acknowledged in the project's "Song Around the World" story

### What Great Looks Like

A Solution Builder who writes clean, accessible code. Who documents their AI workflow for others to learn from. Who reviews others' work constructively. Who builds for the end user — the farmer, the teacher, the health worker — not for the benchmark.

---

## Reviewer

> *The quality bar that keeps the music in tune.*

### What You Do

- Review Pull Requests for quality, correctness, and accessibility
- Provide constructive, specific feedback
- Help maintain coding standards and project conventions
- Mentor Solution Builders through the review process
- Triage issues and help organize work

### Prerequisites

- Active Solution Builder with **5+ merged PRs** across AI4Change projects
- Demonstrated ability to give constructive, clear feedback
- Understanding of the project's architecture and patterns
- Nominated by a Maintainer or self-nominated with Maintainer approval

### Responsibilities

- Review PRs within 72 hours (async is fine)
- Be constructive: explain *why*, not just *what* to change
- Approve only when the contribution is genuinely ready
- Escalate concerns to Maintainers when needed

### Recognition

- Listed as a Reviewer in project docs
- Reviewer badge in GitHub Discussions
- Voice in project direction discussions

### What Great Looks Like

A Reviewer whose feedback makes other contributors better. Who catches bugs and accessibility issues before they ship. Who mentors through reviews — treating each PR as a teaching opportunity, not just a gate.

---

## Maintainer

> *The stewards who keep the songs going.*

### What You Do

- Maintain one or more AI4Change repositories
- Merge Pull Requests and manage releases
- Set project direction in collaboration with the community
- Ensure the project stays aligned with AI4Change's mission
- Onboard and mentor new contributors
- Participate in governance decisions

### Prerequisites

- Active Reviewer with sustained contribution over **3+ months**
- Deep understanding of the project and its user community
- Demonstrated judgment in code review and community interaction
- Nominated by existing Maintainer, ratified by the Steward (or future Council)

### Responsibilities

- Respond to issues and PRs within 1 week
- Keep the project's roadmap and documentation current
- Make decisions transparently with public reasoning
- Mentor Reviewers and Solution Builders
- Attend monthly Maintainer sync (async-friendly)

### Recognition

- Listed as a Maintainer in project README and org docs
- Maintainer badge in GitHub Discussions
- Voting rights in governance decisions
- Eligible for Advisory Circle / Community Council

### What Great Looks Like

A Maintainer who runs a welcoming, high-quality project. Who makes decisions transparently. Who grows the next generation of Maintainers. Who never loses sight of the communities the project serves.

---

## Ambassador

> *The voice that carries the song to the world.*

### What You Do

- Represent AI4Change in your community, region, or professional network
- Help communities submit problems and understand how AI4Change works
- Organize or support regional hubs
- Speak about AI4Change at events, meetups, and conferences
- Translate and localize content for your region
- Connect AI4Change with aligned organizations

### Prerequisites

- Active participation in AI4Change in any role
- Strong connection to a community, region, or professional network
- Ability to communicate AI4Change's mission clearly and authentically
- Self-nominated or nominated by a Maintainer

### Responsibilities

- Represent AI4Change's values and voice accurately
- Share updates from your community monthly
- Respect the communities you serve — never overstate AI4Change's impact
- Provide feedback to the core team on how the initiative can better serve your community

### Recognition

- Listed as an Ambassador on the website and org docs
- Ambassador badge in GitHub Discussions
- Invited to quarterly Ambassador roundtables
- Supported with presentation materials and talking points

### What Great Looks Like

An Ambassador who authentically connects AI4Change with communities that need it. Who listens more than they pitch. Who brings real problems back to the community. Who makes people feel welcome and heard.

## Role Transitions

Roles aren't gatekept — they're earned through contribution. Here's how transitions work:

| Transition | How It Happens |
|:-----------|:--------------|
| Newcomer to Problem Submitter | Submit your first problem |
| Newcomer to Solution Builder | Get your first PR merged |
| Solution Builder to Reviewer | 5+ merged PRs + Maintainer nomination |
| Reviewer to Maintainer | 3+ months active + Maintainer nomination + Steward ratification |
| Any role to Ambassador | Active participation + community connection + self/Maintainer nomination |

### Stepping Down

Life changes. If you need to step back from a role:
- Let a Maintainer know (no explanation needed)
- Your contributions remain credited
- You can return to any role when you're ready
- No judgment, ever. Contributing when you can is enough.

---

## A Note on Titles

These roles exist to clarify expectations and recognize contributions — not to create hierarchy. A Problem Submitter who deeply understands their community's challenges is contributing as much as a Maintainer who reviews code.

In AI4Change, **every musician matters**. The song needs all of them.
