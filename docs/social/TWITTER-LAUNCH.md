## The Thread

### Tweet 1 — The Hook

> What if AI could be the great equalizer?
>
> Not a tool for the privileged few. Not another chatbot for your to-do list.
>
> But a force that lets a farmer in Kenya do the same analysis as a Fortune 500 company.
>
> That's @ai4changeorg. A thread. 🧵

---

### Tweet 2 — The Origin Story

> In 2005, @PlayingForChange filmed musicians on street corners across the globe — each playing their part of "Stand By Me."
>
> None of them had met. But layered together, they created something no single studio could.
>
> We asked: what if we could do that with solutions instead of songs?

---

### Tweet 3 — The Problem

> Right now, the people who need AI the most are the least likely to shape how it's used.
>
> A community health worker tracking outbreaks on paper.
> A teacher personalizing lessons for 50 kids with zero budget.
> A farmer watching crops fail to unpredictable weather.
>
> AI could help them. Today. Not in 10 years.

---

### Tweet 4 — The Parallel

> Playing for Change proved something radical:
>
> When you remove the barriers, ordinary people create extraordinary things.
>
> A guitarist in New Orleans + a drummer in Mumbai + a choir in Soweto = a song no studio could produce.
>
> AI4Change applies the same logic — but with code, not chords.

---

### Tweet 5 — How It Works

> Here's how AI4Change works:
>
> 1. Real people submit real problems
> 2. The community signals what matters most
> 3. Contributors from around the world build together
> 4. Each uses AI as their instrument
> 5. The solution ships — open source, free, forever
>
> We call each project a "song." 🎵

---

### Tweet 6 — What Makes This Different

> This isn't a hackathon that builds for 48 hours and forgets.
>
> This isn't a nonprofit that needs a funding cycle.
>
> This isn't an AI ethics org that writes papers.
>
> We start with the problem. We build in the open. And we ship solutions communities can actually use.
>
> #OpenSource
